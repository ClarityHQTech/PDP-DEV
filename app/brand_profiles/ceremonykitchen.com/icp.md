## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY  
  
### Market Thesis  
  
India's artisanal chocolate and premium food-gifting category sits at the convergence of three structural tailwinds: the premiumization of confectionery consumption, the ritualization of everyday eating, and the professionalization of corporate gifting. The India artisanal chocolate market reached USD 248.9M in 2025 with a projected CAGR of 11.52% through 2034, while India's corporate gifting market is valued at approximately ₹14,000 crore in 2025 and is projected to nearly double by 2030. Cérémony/ITK's "ritual-first" positioning, clean-label formulation (6 ingredients, no preservatives), and Le Cordon Bleu pedigree create a defensible niche at the intersection of these three growth vectors — provided the brand resolves critical gaps in competitive benchmarking, revenue KPIs, and geographic scalability.  
  
### ICP Snapshot  
  
Upper-middle-class, college-educated consumers (ages 25–50) in Delhi NCR who are well-travelled, appreciate clean-ingredient indulgence, and value ritual over routine; plus B2B institutional buyers (HR leaders, law-firm partners, luxury developers) placing bespoke gifting orders ≥₹50K with an emphasis on artisanal craftsmanship, traceable sourcing, and "quiet luxury" packaging.

## 2. COMPANY OVERVIEW & CONTEXT  
  
### 2.1 Brand Identity  
  
In The Know Kitchen (ITK) is a luxury artisan gifts and handcrafted treats brand operating under two identities — **ITK** as the parent artisan kitchen brand and **Cérémony** as the ritual-first food brand extension. The brand offers thoughtful corporate gifting solutions that are handcrafted and sustainable for every occasion, with a curated pantry spanning chocolate truffles, onion chilli jam, tomato sauce, buckwheat granola, pecan granola, and handcrafted crackers.
